What is the LU Decomposition?

Introduced in 1948 by Alan Turing, the LU decomposition is a technique of solving linear systems by factorizing a square matrix A into two triangular matrices: one upper (U) and one lower (L). The upper triangle is a reduced row echelon form containing all linear entry pivot columns, while lower triangle encodres encodes all elementary operations on A.
